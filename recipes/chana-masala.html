Chana Masala

Description

Chana Masala is a popular North Indian dish with tender chickpeas cooked in warming spices in lightly caramelized onion and tangy tomato sauce. A restaurant-favorite dish that is simple to make at home. Serve with steamed rice and raita for a hearty plant-based meal.

Ingredients

    Pressure cooking dry chickpeas

  • 1 cup dry chickpeas
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon Kosher Salt

    • Other Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoon ghee
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 large yellow onion(finely diced)
  • 2 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 2 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 green chilli(slit in the middle)
  • 2 plum tomatoes(finely diced)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1-2 tablespoon chana masala spice blend
  • 1 and half teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 cup water

    • Garnish

  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon kasoori methi

Steps to prepare

  1. Soak white chickpeas in 2 cups of water overnight or for 8-10 hours. Drain out all the water.
  2. Add chickpeas along with 2 cups of water and salt. Pressure cook for 35 minutes followed by natural pressure release. (Note: Optionally, add a tea bag to give the dish a rich smoky flavor and deep color. Discard once the chickpeas are cooked)
  3. Heat ghee in a medium skillet. Add bay leaf and onions. Saut√© on medium heat until the onions turn light golden brown, about 8 to 10 minutes. Use a lid to speed up cooking while mixing in the onions frequently. Add ginger paste, garlic paste, and green chili. Mix well and cook for another minute.
  4. Add tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes stirring a few times until the tomatoes are soft and mushy.
  5. Mix well with chana masala spice blend, salt, turmeric, and red chili powder. Add boiled chickpeas along with the water from pressure cooking. Add one cup of water(more or less) to bring the curry to your desired consistency.
  6. Simmer for 5 minutes and garnish with Kasoori methi and cilantro. Serve with steamed basmati rice or paratha.
